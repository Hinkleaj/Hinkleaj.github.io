Hello, I'm Aaron Hinkle.

Originally from the mountains of Western Montana in the United States, I have spent the last eight years living in North Rhine-Westphalia in Western Germany to study mechanical engineering and robotics.

I have been making and building things for more than 20 years so I have extensive experience with power tools and conventional manufacturing equipment. In addition to my practical manufacturing experience, I am skilled in 3D CAD modeling and simulation as well as mechanics, including a number of technical fortes like engineering design and FEA validation.

I also graduated from FabAcademy in 2022, adding the digital fabrication toolbox to my repertoire. This includes familiarity with 3D printing, CNC machining, electronics design and production and embedded programming, as well as a number of soft skills such as time and project management.

My love for making things is matched only by my love for teaching. I have formal training in education and I have been teaching or tutoring professionally for over ten years. I currently work as a Research Associate in Mechanics at the Rhine-Waal University of Applied Science. My academic and research endeavors primarily revolve around the integration of bionic principles into manufacturing, aiming to advance the field through innovative research and practical applications.

TseNC-Pro CNC Machine

What:

  • Commissioned redesign of an open source CNC machine to solve the problems of the original
  • Automate chip removal in the form of a flood coolant system and enclosure
  • The new machine should be able to cut metal all day long without supervision

    • How:

  • Designed using Solidworks
  • Coolant system pushes chips to one end of the bed where the coolant is filtered and recirculated
  • Hanging the gantry below the cutting bed prevents chip buildup on the linear rails
  • Created a comprehensive BOM including Make-or-Buy decisions and vendor sourcing for COTs parts

    • Results:

  • Created engineering drawings for over 100 parts
  • Created an assembly manual with each step illustrated using exploded CAD assemblies
  • Solved the problems of the original, improved the functionality of the original, and gave the improved version back to the open source community

    What:

  • Co-project with Sophia D√∂ring aimed at implementing three different numerical methods for solving IVPs
  • Comparing efficiency and accuracy of solvers using different types of test problems with known analytical solutions
  • Implement project in MATLAB

    • How:

  • Methods: constant step size implicit Euler solver using Newton-Raphson method, adaptive step algorithm based on ratio between first and second derivatives, and adaptive step algorithm based on minimizing MSE
  • Test problems: Linear differential equation, a nonlinear differential equation, and an ODE with variable coefficients

    Fab Academy

    Fab Academy is a multi-disciplinary, hands-on, five-month program focused on designing and prototyping digital fabrication projects.The pedagogical foundations come from MIT's renowned rapid prototyping course, "How to Make (Almost) Anything". It is distinguished by its unique approach to education, which operates on two levels: a virtual campus connecting over 250 students from over 50 cities worldwide, and local nodes where students engage in person, fostering both global connectivity and local engagement.

    The curriculum is structured around 20 week-long intensive seminars that cover both core and interdisciplinary aspects of digital manufacturing and rapid prototyping. Students plan and execute a new project each week and document it comprehensively on a personal GIT repo and website, resulting in individual portfolios of technical accomplishments. The course culminates in a final project, where participants apply the breadth of their learning to bring an idea into practical reality. The goal is to democratize innovative solutions that advocate for more sustainable systems.
