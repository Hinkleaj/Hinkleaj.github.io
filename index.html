Summary

Aaron Jay Hinkle

Originally from the mountains of Western Montana in the United States, I have spent the last eight years living in North Rhine-Westphalia in Western Germany to study mechanical engineering and robotics.

I have been making and building things for more than 20 years so I have extensive experience with power tools and conventional manufacturing equipment. In addition to my practical manufacturing experience, I am skilled in 3D CAD modeling and simulation as well as mechanics, including a number of technical fortes like engineering design and FEA validation.

I also graduated from FabAcademy in 2022, adding the digital fabrication toolbox to my repertoire. This includes familiarity with 3D printing, CNC machining, electronics design and production and embedded programming, as well as a number of soft skills such as time and project management.

My love for making things is matched only by my love for teaching. I have formal training in education and I have been teaching or tutoring professionally for over ten years. My most recent position was as a Research Associate in Mechanics at the Rhine-Waal University of Applied Science. My academic and research endeavors primarily revolve around the integration of bionic principles into manufacturing, aiming to advance the field through innovative research and practical applications.

Fab Academy

Class of 2022

Fab Academy is an intensive, hands-on program focused on digital fabrication. The pedagogical foundations come from MIT's renowned rapid prototyping course, "How to Make (Almost) Anything". The curriculum is structured around 20, week-long, intensive seminars that cover both core and interdisciplinary aspects of rapid prototyping and the technologies and workflows involved. Students plan and execute a new project each week and comprehensively document it on a personal website and GIT repo, resulting in unique individual portfolios of technical accomplishments. The course culminates in a final project, where participants apply the breadth of their learning to bring a big idea into reality.
